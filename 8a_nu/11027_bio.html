Recently I have found redpointing an extremely useful tool to improving my maximum strength so I am finding the hardest routes that challenge me the most in areas I am not good at (like short powerful routes) to challenge me...

8a Questionnaire
What do you like the most in your climbing life?I love climbing in different places around the world, the people you meet in these places and the crazy routes I get to climb in them. I love pushing my physical and mental barrier when onsighting and redpointing hard routes and boulders. Training for me is almost as enjoyable as going out and climbing on real rock and the feeling of knowing your getting stronger everyday you train is unbeatable...
 
What is a memorable story in your climbing life?A memorable moment in my climbing life was on my 2nd trip to kalymnos with my friend Mark Jarvi. He belayed me on a 40m 7b+. We weren't sure if we had a long enough rope but I went for it anyway. I climbed it as the sun set over the neighbouring island of Telendos and although I don't remember any of the moves, I remember the beautiful colour of the rock and the other climbers climbing in the Grande Grotta who all looked like tiny ants. This is my most memorable moment in ym climbing life...
 
Who are your climbing heroes and why?My climbing heroes are all my friends i meet on my climbing travels not to mention the people who generally push their physical and mental barriers in the sport no matter what grade they climb...

Which climbing destinations could you recommend and why?My three top climbing destinations are kalymnos, Ceuse and Rodellar. They are great because they suit nearly every climber there is, whether your old or young, climb 9a or 5a, they are top destinations for everyone and they have amazing social scenes too...
